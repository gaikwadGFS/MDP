Services Offered

Consultation Type

Buying or selling property involves preparing and validating legal documents. Advocates can assist with:

Property Verification:

Assistance in verifying the legal status of a property, ensuring there are no pending disputes, or that the title deed is clean and legitimate.

Agreement Drafting:

Advocates can help draft legal documents such as sale agreements, lease agreements, or MoUs tailored to the transaction.

Dispute Resolution:

Support in resolving disputes over property ownership, rental disagreements, or boundary conflicts.

Registration Assistance:

Helping clients during the property registration process, including preparing documentation and dealing with government offices.

Tax and Compliance Advice:

Providing advice on tax-related aspects like capital gains tax, stamp duty, and other property-related levies.

Fee Structure

Transparency in pricing is vital for user trust. Advocates can display their charges for different services:

Consultation Fee:

Fixed fee for a one-time consultation session.

Hourly Fee:

Hourly billing for more extensive or ongoing legal support.

Fixed Charges for Services:

Predefined charges for specific tasks such as agreement drafting or registration assistance.

Online Appointment Option

Available dates and times.
Payment options for booking confirmation.
Video or in-person consultation preference.

Other Specialized Services

Depending on the expertise of the advocate, they may also offer niche services, such as:

Real Estate Due Diligence:

Comprehensive analysis of the property, including legal, financial, and regulatory checks.

Litigation Services:

Representation in property-related legal disputes in courts or tribunals.

Partition or Inheritance Advice:

Legal support for dividing ancestral property among heirs.
