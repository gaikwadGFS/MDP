Masonry Construction

Masonry construction uses bricks, stones, or concrete blocks to create strong and durable walls. While it offers excellent weather resistance and thermal efficiency, it requires skilled labor and is generally more time-consuming and costly.

Timber Frame Construction

Timber frame uses wooden beams and trusses for structural support, making it for residential buildings due to its aesthetic appeal and design flexibility. It requires regular maintenance to prevent moisture and pest issues.

Steel Frame Construction

Steel frame uses a skeleton of steel columns and beams to support buildings, making it ideal for high-rise structures due to its strength-to-weight ratio.It offers durability and fire resistance, it can be more expensive upfront than traditional materials like wood or masonry.

Modular Construction

Modular involves prefabricating building sections in a factory & transporting them to the site for assembly, significantly reducing construction time & labor costs while allowing for flexible designs suitable for various uses (residential & commercial spaces).
